Three UX fixes needed in the Pillar dashboard:

Fix 1: Show site build status in the dashboard navbar, not a dead-end page.

Instead of showing "Site is building. Try again in a moment." on a blank page that the customer has to manually refresh, add a persistent status indicator in the top navbar of the Pillar dashboard:

While deploying: Show a small banner or badge: "Your website is being set up..." with a subtle spinner. The customer can continue using other parts of the dashboard while this happens.
When done: The banner changes to: "Your website is live! Visit it →" with a link. This stays visible for the session or until dismissed.
If it fails after 5 minutes: "Website setup encountered an issue. Click here to retry." with a retry button.
The customer should never land on a dead-end "try again" screen. They should always be able to do other things while the site deploys.

Fix 2: After the site is built, "Website" in the sidebar should open a settings/edit page — not restart the interview.

Once the interview is complete and the site is live, clicking "Website" in the sidebar should show a Website Settings page with:

Site URL section: Shows orgname.mypillar.co with an "Edit" button next to the subdomain part. Clicking edit lets them change orgname to something else. When saved, Pillar updates DNS and the stored URL. Below that, a "Connect custom domain" option for customers who own their own domain.
Site Content section: Shows all their interview answers in editable form fields grouped by category:
Organization (name, short name, tagline, org type)
Contact Info (email, phone, address, mailing address)
Social Media (Facebook, Instagram)
Features (blog on/off, newsletter on/off, sponsors on/off, vendors on/off, tickets on/off)
Stats (the 4 homepage stats, editable)
Partners (add/remove/edit)
Meeting Schedule (day, time, location)
Each section has a "Save" button. When saved, Pillar rebuilds the payload from the updated values and calls POST /api/pillar/setup on the customer's site again. The site updates in seconds.
AI Update button: A single button that says "Update with AI." When clicked, it opens a chat where the customer can say things like "Change our tagline to 'Building Community Together'" or "Add a new partner: Norwin Lions Club - Service organization." The AI parses the request, updates the stored answers, rebuilds the payload, and calls the setup API. The customer sees a confirmation of what changed.
"Start Over" option: At the very bottom, a small link: "Want to redo the interview from scratch?" This is the only way to restart the full interview. It should require confirmation: "This will reset all your answers. Are you sure?"
Fix 3: The 2-minute "Site is building" hang.

Two minutes is too long for what should be a simple API call. Debug why it's taking so long:

If Pillar is deploying a new app instance: that should take 30-60 seconds max. If it's longer, something is wrong with the deployment pipeline.
If the site already exists (like Discover Irwin): there's nothing to deploy. Just call the setup API. That takes under 5 seconds.
Add a timeout: if the deployment hasn't completed after 3 minutes, show an error with a retry button instead of leaving the customer staring at a spinner forever.
