# Pillar — Business Plan

**Tagline:** Your organization, on autopilot.  
**Domain:** mypillar.co  
**Stage:** Pre-revenue, product built, ready to acquire first customers  
**Date:** March 2026

---

## 1. What Pillar Is

Pillar is a SaaS platform for civic organizations — Masonic lodges, HOAs, VFW posts, Rotary clubs, PTAs, nonprofits, and community associations. It handles the administrative and digital infrastructure that volunteers are currently doing manually: building and maintaining a website, posting to social media, managing events, routing board approvals, and keeping contacts organized.

The core promise: an organization administrator can set Pillar up once and then not think about their digital operations again.

---

## 2. The Problem

Civic organizations are run by volunteers who have real jobs. The person managing the HOA website is also a nurse, or an accountant, or a retiree who doesn't want to learn Squarespace. They're managing events on paper, sending newsletters over email chains, updating a website they hate, and copying-and-pasting social posts manually — or they've just stopped doing it altogether.

The existing tools (Squarespace, Wix, Mailchimp, Hootsuite) were not built for them. They require skill, time, and ongoing maintenance. Pillar was.

**The real competition isn't other software.** It's doing nothing, paying a college student $200 to build a site that never gets updated, or paying a web agency $300/month for a glorified template.

---

## 3. Who We Serve

**Primary targets (early):**
- HOA board presidents and secretaries (1–10M+ HOAs in the US)
- Masonic lodge secretaries (over 10,000 lodges in the US alone)
- VFW and American Legion post commanders (~12,000 VFW posts)
- PTA presidents (25,000+ PTAs)
- Rotary, Lions, and Kiwanis clubs (~45,000 clubs combined)
- Small nonprofits with under $500K annual budget

**Customer profile:**
- Age: 40–70
- Not technical
- Volunteer or part-time administrator
- Currently spending 3–8 hours/month on tasks Pillar automates
- Budget authority: usually under $200/month before needing board approval

**Beachhead market:** Masonic lodges. They are well-networked, have a known secretary role, run predictable annual event calendars, and actively share tools across lodges through grand lodge networks.

## 5. How We Acquire Customers

**Phase 1 — Direct outreach (months 1–6)**
- Cold email to lodge secretaries, HOA board presidents, VFW commanders
- LinkedIn outreach targeting "HOA president," "lodge secretary," etc.
- Posting value-add content in Facebook groups for HOA boards, civic orgs
- Target: 5–10 new trials per week from outreach alone

**Phase 2 — Content and SEO (months 3–12)**
- Blog content targeting "HOA website software," "Masonic lodge website," "PTA newsletter tool"
- These are low-competition keywords with high commercial intent
- Long-term: SEO drives 30–40% of trials

**Phase 3 — Partnerships (months 6–18)**
- Reach grand lodge networks (one deal = access to hundreds of lodges)
- Partner with HOA management companies who serve dozens of HOAs
- Reach nonprofit associations and umbrella orgs
- Target: one partnership deal = 20–100 customers

**Phase 4 — Paid (months 9+)**
- Facebook ads targeting civic org administrators by interest and role
- Google ads targeting high-intent search terms
- Only once organic and outreach are converting well

## 6. Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | What They Do | Why Pillar Wins |
|------------|--------------|-----------------|
| Squarespace/Wix | Generic website builders | Require skill and ongoing manual work; not org-specific |
| Wild Apricot | Membership management for nonprofits | $60–$900/mo, complex, no automation |
| MemberClicks | Association management software | Enterprise pricing ($300–$1,000+/mo), overkill |
| Mailchimp | Email newsletters | Single-channel, still manual, no website |
| Hootsuite | Social media scheduling | Still manual scheduling, not connected to org calendar |
| A web agency | Custom site + maintenance | $300–$1,000/mo, slow, doesn't integrate anything |

**Pillar's position:** The only platform built specifically for civic organization administrators that combines website, events, social, board approvals, and contacts — at a price a volunteer can approve without a board vote.

## 7. Operations

**Infrastructure:**
- Hosted on Replit (deployments), moving to dedicated hosting at scale
- PostgreSQL database (Replit-managed)
- AI: OpenAI (gpt-4o-mini) for website generation and social content; Anthropic (claude-haiku) for support chat
- Payments: Stripe with 14-day free trial and webhook-managed subscriptions
- Domains: Porkbun API for custom domain purchasing

**Current team:** Founder only  
**Year 1 team additions:** Customer Success (month 4), Growth (month 7), Outreach (month 9)

## 9. Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|------------|
| Civic orgs don't want to pay for software | Medium | 14-day trial removes friction; $29 is under discretionary spend threshold for most |
| Low technical adoption (customers can't set it up) | Medium | Customer success role exists for this reason; onboarding is guided |
| Churn because orgs go dormant | Medium | Annual billing option at discount reduces this; check-ins at 30/60/90 days |
| AI content quality complaints | Low | Customers review before publishing; model can be improved |
| Competitive response from Wild Apricot | Low | They serve a different (larger, more complex) customer |
