# Pillar — Team Roles, Job Descriptions & Daily Tasks

> **Philosophy:** Every role below is designed to be filled by a part-time contractor working remotely. You (the founder) approve anything that costs money or changes the product. Everyone else executes inside defined lanes.

---

## Role 1: Customer Success Coordinator

**Title:** Customer Success Coordinator  
**Type:** Part-time contractor, 15–20 hrs/week  
**Pay range:** $18–$24/hr (~$1,200–$1,800/mo)  
**Hire when:** MRR hits $3,000 (roughly 50 customers)  
**Reports to:** Founder  
**Primary tools:** Pillar admin panel, support ticket queue, email, Notion

### What they own
- Every support ticket from open to resolved
- New customer onboarding (first 7 days)
- Churn prevention (customers who go silent or cancel)
- Monthly check-ins with active customers

### What they do NOT own
- Product decisions
- Refunds over $50 (escalate to founder)
- Anything involving the codebase

### Job Description
You are the first human voice a Pillar customer hears. Your job is to make sure every organization that signs up actually gets their website live, their events posted, and their board approvals flowing — within their first week. You answer questions, troubleshoot problems, and proactively reach out to customers who look stuck. You are not a tech support agent; you are a success partner who knows the product cold and helps customers get real outcomes.

### Daily Tasks

**Every morning (30–45 min)**
- [ ] Check the support ticket queue; respond to every open ticket within 2 business hours
- [ ] Check for any new sign-ups from yesterday; send a personal welcome email within 24 hours of sign-up
- [ ] Flag any customer whose trial is ending in 3 days (check admin panel) — send a "how's it going?" message
- [ ] Note any ticket that requires a product bug or feature request — log it for the founder

**Every afternoon (30 min)**
- [ ] Follow up on any ticket marked "waiting on customer" for more than 24 hours
- [ ] Check for customers in trial who haven't completed onboarding step 1 (website build) — send a nudge
- [ ] Review any cancellation or downgrade that happened that day; log the stated reason

**Every Friday (1 hr)**
- [ ] Write a short weekly summary: tickets resolved, new customers onboarded, churned customers + reasons, patterns noticed
- [ ] Send the summary to the founder via email or Notion
- [ ] Flag the 2–3 customers most at risk of churning next week

### Onboarding Script (first contact)
> "Hi [Name], I'm [Your Name] with Pillar. I help new organizations get set up. You signed up [X] days ago — have you had a chance to build your website yet? I can walk you through it in about 10 minutes if that helps."

## Role 2: Growth & Content Coordinator

**Title:** Growth & Content Coordinator  
**Type:** Part-time contractor, 10–15 hrs/week  
**Pay range:** $20–$30/hr (~$1,200–$2,000/mo)  
**Hire when:** MRR hits $5,000 (roughly 80 customers) or founder wants to grow faster  
**Reports to:** Founder  
**Primary tools:** Buffer/Later or direct posting, Canva, Google Analytics, Pillar's own landing page, email newsletter tool

### What they own
- Pillar's own social media presence (LinkedIn, Facebook, X)
- Written content (blog posts, case studies, email newsletters)
- SEO — ensuring the landing page ranks for searches like "HOA website software," "civic organization tools," "Masonic lodge website"
- Paid ad experiments (small budget, founder approves spend)

### What they do NOT own
- The product itself
- Customer success conversations
- Any spend over $100 without founder approval

### Job Description
You grow Pillar's audience and turn that audience into trial sign-ups. You understand that Pillar's customers are civic organization administrators — volunteer board presidents, HOA secretaries, lodge commanders — not tech people. Your content speaks their language. You write about the problems they have, not the technology that solves them. You find communities where these people gather (Facebook groups for HOA boards, Masonic lodge forums, VFW post networks) and you show up there with value, not ads.

### Daily Tasks

**Every morning (30 min)**
- [ ] Post one piece of content to LinkedIn and Facebook — rotate between: tip posts, "did you know" posts, short case study angles, Pillar feature highlights
- [ ] Respond to any comments or messages on Pillar's social profiles
- [ ] Check Google Search Console — note any keywords trending up or down

**Every week (Mondays, 2 hrs)**
- [ ] Write one long-form piece: blog post, email newsletter, or SEO landing page draft (e.g., "Best website tools for HOAs in 2026")
- [ ] Research 3–5 Facebook groups, Reddit threads, or forum communities where target customers hang out — plan a value-add comment or post for later that week
- [ ] Review last week's sign-up source data — which channels are driving trials?

**Every week (Fridays, 1 hr)**
- [ ] Pull weekly metrics: website visitors, trial sign-ups, conversion rate, social reach
- [ ] Write a one-paragraph summary of what worked, what didn't, what to try next week
- [ ] Share the summary with the founder

### Content Pillars (what to post about)
1. **Problems first** — "Managing an HOA newsletter manually takes 4 hours a month. Here's how to get it to 20 minutes."
2. **Civic organization spotlights** — Highlight the kind of orgs Pillar is built for
3. **Product proof** — Show Pillar features in action (screenshots, short demos)
4. **Community tips** — Generic advice for running civic orgs (even non-product content)
5. **Social proof** — When real customers say real things, post it

## Role 3: Outreach & Partnerships Coordinator

**Title:** Outreach & Partnerships Coordinator  
**Type:** Part-time contractor, 10 hrs/week  
**Pay range:** $20–$28/hr (~$800–$1,200/mo)  
**Hire when:** MRR hits $7,500 — growth channel proven, ready to scale with direct outreach  
**Reports to:** Founder  
**Primary tools:** Email, LinkedIn, Google Sheets (prospect tracker), Pillar admin panel

### What they own
- Building and working a list of target organizations (HOAs, lodges, VFW posts, nonprofits, PTAs, Rotary clubs)
- Cold outreach — email and LinkedIn — to organization administrators
- Partnership conversations with umbrella orgs (state Masonic grand lodges, HOA management companies, nonprofit networks)
- Tracking responses and booking demo calls for the founder

### What they do NOT own
- Closing deals (founder closes or the product closes itself via trial)
- Making commitments about the product
- Sending any email that hasn't been approved by the founder as a template

### Job Description
You find the people who need Pillar before they know Pillar exists. Your targets are not random — they are the secretary of a Masonic lodge who just got elected and doesn't know how to update the lodge website, or the HOA board president who is printing paper newsletters in 2026. You find them in directories, Facebook groups, LinkedIn, and public organization registries. You send them short, honest, human emails that acknowledge their specific situation. You don't pitch features — you describe their problem back to them and offer to show them something that helps.

### Daily Tasks

**Every morning (1 hr)**
- [ ] Research and add 10–15 new prospects to the outreach tracker (name, org, role, email if findable, notes on their current setup)
- [ ] Send 15–20 outreach emails using approved templates — personalize the first line for each
- [ ] Log all responses: interested, not interested, no reply, bounced

**Every afternoon (30 min)**
- [ ] Follow up with anyone who opened but didn't reply (3-day follow-up rule)
- [ ] Reply to any interested prospects — goal is to get them to start a free trial or book a call with the founder
- [ ] Update the prospect tracker with current status

**Every week (Fridays, 30 min)**
- [ ] Report: how many contacted, how many replied, how many trials started from outreach, how many bounced
- [ ] Flag any partnership leads (umbrella orgs, associations, management companies) for the founder to handle directly

### Outreach Template (approved starting point)
> **Subject:** [Lodge/HOA name] — quick question
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I noticed [Lodge/HOA name] has [observation — e.g., an outdated website / no website listed]. I run a platform called Pillar that a lot of civic organizations use to keep their site and member communications running automatically.
>
> Not sure if it's a fit, but wanted to ask — is managing your org's online presence something that takes more time than it should?
>
> Happy to send over more info or just let you poke around for free.
>
> — [Name]

## Role 4: Operations & Admin Coordinator *(future hire)*

**Title:** Operations & Admin Coordinator  
**Type:** Part-time contractor, 5–10 hrs/week  
**Pay range:** $16–$22/hr (~$400–$900/mo)  
**Hire when:** MRR hits $10,000 — operations complexity justifies it  
**Reports to:** Founder  
**Primary tools:** Stripe dashboard, Pillar admin panel, email, Notion

### What they own
- Monitoring failed payments and following up with customers
- Tracking subscription changes, upgrades, downgrades
- Keeping the Notion operating dashboard up to date (MRR, churn, active customers)
- Handling routine billing questions that don't require founder judgment

### What they do NOT own
- Issuing refunds (escalate to founder)
- Any product or technical decisions
- Customer success conversations (that's Role 1)

### Daily Tasks

**Every morning (20 min)**
- [ ] Check Stripe for any failed payments from the last 24 hours — log them and send a polite payment failure email to the customer
- [ ] Check for any new upgrades or downgrades — log them in the MRR tracker
- [ ] Flag anything unusual to the founder (large refund request, dispute, unusual activity)

**Every week (Mondays, 30 min)**
- [ ] Update the weekly MRR snapshot in Notion
- [ ] Reconcile any subscription changes against the previous week
- [ ] Flag any customer who has failed payment for 7+ days — escalate for potential cancellation
